// Biography

Some pedal steel players fill a role. Kevin "The General" Neal defines the sound of a record.

A university-trained musician with an instinct for the spaces between notes, The General has built one of the most respected reputations in Canadian country music — not through self-promotion, but through the undeniable authority of his playing. His stage name wasn't chosen. It was earned.

Kevin has shared stages with Darius Rucker, Midland, and some of the biggest names in North American country, performing at landmark venues and festivals including the Calgary Stampede, Mariposa Folk Festival, and major festival stages in Norway — as well as the Grand Ole Opry in Nashville. His touring footprint spans Canada, the United States, and Europe.

In the studio, Kevin is equally at home. He has recorded with artists across multiple genres and continents — including appearing on Jade Eagleson's recording of Steal My Girl, bringing his signature tone to one of the most recognizable pop melodies of the last decade. His session work reflects the same quality that has made him a trusted first call for artists at every level.

Twice nominated for CCMA Pedal Steel Player of the Year, Kevin's credentials come from the people who know the instrument best — fellow musicians and industry peers who recognize the rare combination of technical mastery and musical intelligence he brings to every room.

His approach is systematic, tactical, and unfailingly musical. He reads a room, serves the song, and elevates the artist. That's why they call him The General.
